<p><strong><u>Department Vision</u></strong>
</p>
<p>At St. Gabriel’s Primary School, we shape our young Gabrielites into confident,
future-ready problem solvers through an inclusive and technology-driven
mathematical journey.</p>
<p>Our department vision is anchored on three core pillars:</p>
<ol data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Discover &amp; Learn through Inquiry:</strong> We create structural
and exploratory opportunities for students to actively discover and internalize
mathematical concepts through guided inquiry.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Future-Ready &amp; Motivated Problem Solvers:</strong> We nurture
confident, critical problem solvers and motivated lifelong learners by
seamlessly embedding Educational Technology (EdTech) into everyday mathematical
practices.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Teacher Excellence &amp; Continuous Growth:</strong> We provide
robust pathways for our teachers to excel in their distinct areas of expertise
and pursue lifelong innovative learning.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p><strong><u>Pedagogical Approach &amp; Core Curriculum</u></strong>
</p>
<p><strong>1. Joyful, Active, and Meaningful Learning</strong>
</p>
<p>To bring mathematics to life beyond text formulas, our school curriculum
integrates dynamic, real-world instruction mapped across all levels:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Math in the News:</strong> Implemented every semester across the
levels. These activities deliberately infuse critical mathematical reasoning
strategies alongside National Education (NE) messages and foundational
Financial Literacy.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Active Learning Experiences:</strong> Kinesthetic, tactile activities
designed to be infused directly into the curriculum. These experiences
anchor abstract calculations into tangible, moving realities.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>5 Practices for Orchestrating Meaningful Math Discussions:</strong> Teachers
implement this instructional framework to guide classroom dialogue, elevating
students’ reasoning and deepening underlying conceptual clarity.</p>
</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>2. EdTech Capabilities</strong>
</p>
<p>Technology serves as a primary capability multiplier to advance students’
engagement, motivation, and self-directed competence in the modern digital
landscape:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Interactive Formative Assessment:</strong> EdTech tools such as
ClassPoint are infused directly into PowerPoint slides to secure immediate
formative checks on understanding.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Tailored Digital Practice:</strong> KooBits assignments are selectively
distributed at the conclusion of every topic. Higher-Order Thinking (HOT)
problems are intentionally targeted to Higher Ability (HA) students to
consistently stretch individual potential.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Blended Centralized Resources:</strong> Student Learning Space
(SLS) instructional videos and curated digital activities are utilized
for every topic.</p>
</li>
</ul>

<p><strong><u>Key Signature Programmes &amp; Interventions</u></strong>
</p>
<p><strong>1. Differentiated Learning Ecosystem</strong>
</p>
<p>We believe in establishing an inclusive environment that embraces diverse
learning needs through precise, data-informed instruction:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>Learning Support for Mathematics (LSM):</strong> An early intervention
framework providing focused, small-group support to ensure foundational
mathematical mastery for selected P1 to P4 students.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Pull-Out and Remedial Classes:</strong> Differentiated learning
support through focused remedial and pull-out classes for P5 and P6 students.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>The Math Corner:</strong> Interactive mathematical hubs integrated
directly into Primary 1 classrooms, enriching our youngest students' appreciation
of numbers through collaborative games and play-based activities.</p>
</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>2. Nurturing High-Ability Learners (HAL)</strong>
</p>
<p>For students displaying exceptional mathematical aptitude, we provide
rigorous pathways to stretch thinking limits and cultivate advanced problem-solving
dispositions:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p><strong>The E2K Mathematics Programme:</strong> An inquiry-focused curriculum
seamlessly infused directly into the Primary 4 to Primary 6 LMM Curriculum.
To build a blended learning experience, segments of these advanced E2K
lessons are uploaded onto the SLS, encouraging self-directed exploration
and creating valuable classroom time for productive, higher-level mathematical
discussions.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Non-Routine Problem Exposure:</strong> Early introduction to non-routine
mathematical scenarios to stretch conceptual understanding and teach creative
lateral thinking.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>National Mathematical Competitions:</strong> Competitions opportunities
are provided for high-ability students to test their skills against national
standards, including:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>Raffles Mathematics Olympiad (RMO)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>National Mathematical Olympiad of Singapore (NMOS)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Asia-Pacific Mathematical Olympiad for Primary School (APMOPS)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Primary Mathematics Project Competition (Organised by GEB)</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>3. Interdisciplinary Aesthetics Integration</strong>
</p>
<p>We bridge the gap between math and creative arts through vibrant, cross-departmental
collaborations. Our Art with Math curriculum allows students to directly
apply core mathematical concepts, like symmetry, geometric nets, and 3D
figures to design beautiful, physical art pieces.</p>
<p><strong>4. Authentic Learning Journeys</strong>
</p>
<p>In partnership with the Science Department, we get students out of the
classroom to experience the application of mathematics in everyday environments.
Our Primary 3 students embark on a learning journey to the zoo to experience
a specialized out-of-classroom journey focusing on the practical application
of money in real-world contexts.</p>
<p><strong><u>Parental Partnership</u></strong>
</p>
<p>Raising every Gabrielite to succeed and thrive in Mathematics is a team
effort. We bridge the gap between classroom learning and home support through
termly engagement letters, ensuring families stay updated with their child’s
mathematical journey.</p>
